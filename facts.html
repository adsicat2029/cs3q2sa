Siquijor is called "Isla del Fuego"

Spanish colonizers, upon arriving in 1565, saw swarms of fireflies lighting up the molave trees at night, making the island appear to glow. They named it "Isla del Fuego," which translates to "Island of Fire".

Siquijor is full of folklore!

Legends describe the island's origin from a great earthquake and the rise of a mysterious limestone rock. The island is also associated with mythical creatures like the aswang and tikbalang, and sacred sites like the ancient Balete Tree are central to many local stories.

Known as the “Mystic Island” or “Island of Healers”

Siquijor has also been called the “healing island” because of the folk healers and shamans who practice their craft to this day. The island province also has a Healing Festival, celebrated every holy week to show that their process is sacred and meant for good intentions.

Siquijor is super lively!

Siquijor celebrates its annual town fiesta every 4th of October in honor of its patron saint, St. Francis of Assisi. The Dilaab Festival or Fire Festival falls to commemorate Siquijor as Isla de Fuego as what it is called before by Spanish conquistadors.
